!! HEPATITIS

    Inflamattory condition of the liver.

  • Symptoms : Symptoms include abdominal pain, loss of appetite, fatigue, dark urine, pale stool and jaundice.
  • Causes : Causes include viral infection, alcohol abuse and autoimmunity.
  • Treatment : Treatment depends on the type and may include self care practices such as getting adequate rest, plenty of fluids, and avoiding alcohol, antiviral medication or corticosteroids

  • Hep A foodborne

    Hep A foodborne through feces, preventable with vaccine.

  • B bloodborne,

  • Hep C bloodborne

    transmitted through needle sharing. No prevention. Hep C is the leading cause of liver transplants.

    Hep D only infects people with hep B, so prevent hep B and you won't get hep D.

    Hep E pigs may be the primary reservoir. 11% of pig livers show Hep E virus.

    Hep E is highly risky for pregnancy, so, while pregnant, cook pork well or avoid entirely.

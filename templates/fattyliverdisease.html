!! Fatty Liver Disease

  • Fat deposits in liver cause inflammation, which cause liver scarring, which leads to liver failure

  • One week of a fast food-heavy diet can cause pathological liver function tests. One can of soda per day raises fatty liver disease by 45%.

    • There are two main forms of fatty liver disease:

  • fatty liver disease

    • Alcohol-induced fatty liver disease

  • caused by heavy drinking. (Moderate drinking is defined as one drink a day for women and up to two drinks per day for men.) About 5% of people in the U.S. have this form of liver disease.

    • Non-alcohol related fatty liver disease

  • Non-alcohol related fatty liver disease (NAFLD) occurs in people who arenot heavy drinkers. The condition affects one in three adults and one in 10 children in the United States. Researchers havenot found the exact cause of non-alcohol related fatty liver disease. Several factors, such as obesity and diabetes, can increase your risk.
